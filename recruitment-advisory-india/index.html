The Root Problem

Most companies don't have a talent problem.
They have a decision problem.

The failure happens before the first CV is reviewed. The brief is misaligned, the evaluation criteria are wrong, or the wrong stakeholders are making the call. Leadership hiring fails because of process failure, not talent scarcity.

Where hiring decisions break down

Wrong brief. The JD describes the role as it existed, not as it needs to evolve. Every CV looks wrong because the spec is wrong.
Wrong evaluation signals. Brand names, degrees, confident presentation: none predict performance in your context.
Wrong decision-makers. Panels avoid conflict and pick the least objectionable option. Single managers hire people like themselves.
Wrong timeline. Urgency compresses process. Compressed process privileges available candidates over the right ones.
No post-hire accountability. The recruitment partner disappears at offer acceptance. The real test begins after that.
Actual Hiring Failure Pattern

Series B SaaS company, Bangalore. Hired VP Engineering from a unicorn. Strong pedigree, solid interviews. 8 months in: zero shipped features, team attrition at 40%, ₹2.8Cr in runway burned. The problem: candidate was evaluated on past employer brand, not their ability to build without that infrastructure. The brief never tested for resource-constrained execution. Timeline: 18 months to recover (new hire + team rebuild).

Regional Hiring Context

Regional variation is real.
The root cause is the same.

Hiring failure patterns vary by market, but the structural problem remains consistent: speed pressure, wrong evaluation signals, and absent stakeholder alignment. Here's what we see in each major market.

Bangalore · Karnataka

Speed vs. quality

Urgency-driven shortlisting privileges available candidates over the right ones. Brand-name bias (Swiggy, Flipkart, Razorpay) without testing if the experience transfers.

Failure Pattern

Speed pressure → premature shortlisting → underqualified hire → back at start in 90 days with weaker position.

Chennai · Tamil Nadu

Execution depth, leadership gap

Strong operational talent, culture-driven organizations. External leadership hires fail when the organization isn't prepared to receive change. The problem precedes the search.

Failure Pattern

Recruiting for transformation without internal readiness. 12-18 month exit cycle attributed to "culture fit."

Kerala

Talent pool, alignment problem

Experienced professionals returning from Gulf markets with corporate playbooks calibrated for different environments. Contextual adaptability is rarely tested.

Failure Pattern

Gulf returnee or metro hire evaluated on credentials, not context fit. Fails within 12 months.

Delhi NCR · Noida · Delhi

Noise, not depth

India's largest hiring market outside Mumbai. Most fragmented. Title inflation, relationship-driven placements, and candidates who held authority vs. exercised it.

Failure Pattern

Hired for title and brand. Deploys processes, not outcomes. Company waits too long to act. 12 months lost.

The Agency Problem

Most recruitment partners optimise for placement speed.
Not decision quality.

The contingency model creates a structural misalignment. Agencies win when they place fast, not when you hire right. This determines behavior at every step.

No diagnostic phase

Search begins the day the JD arrives. The brief is never challenged. The role definition is never tested. Six months later, the hire fails because the brief was wrong.

Volume over fit

10-15 CVs with no recommendation. You filter. You evaluate fit. The agency optimised for speed, not accuracy. The decision risk sits with you.

Exit at offer acceptance

Success = signed offer. The agency disappears. 30-60-90 day performance, onboarding support, stakeholder alignment: all left to you.

How We Work

Diagnostic first.
Search second.
Accountable throughout.

Every mandate begins with stakeholder alignment. We rebuild the brief when it's wrong. We challenge the timeline when it's compressed. The search begins only after the decision framework is clear.

01

Stakeholder diagnostic

Who decides? What are they optimising for? Where is the misalignment? We map this before the search begins.

02

Brief challenge

Role clarity, seniority calibration, success metrics, compensation benchmarking. Most briefs change at this stage.

03

Scorecard design

What does good look like in 90 days? What competencies matter? How do we test for them? Agreed before search.

04

Assessed shortlist

3-5 candidates. Each tested against the scorecard. Written recommendation with reasoning. Not a ranked list, a decision brief.

05

Offer design

Compensation structure, equity, role scope, reporting lines. We support the negotiation, not just connect you.

06

Post-placement advisory

30-60-90 day check-ins included. Onboarding support, stakeholder feedback, early warning signals. Accountability continues.
